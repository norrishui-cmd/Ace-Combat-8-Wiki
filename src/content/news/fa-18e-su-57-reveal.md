Project Aces kicked off a new wave of aircraft reveals with two dedicated posts, each
introducing a single aircraft with in-engine screenshots.

## F/A-18E Super Hornet — the protagonist's starting aircraft

Described by the developers as a carrier-based multirole fighter built for "naval fleet air
defense to close air support," this revised E-model is pitched around **medium-speed
maneuverability** rather than raw top speed, with an emphasis on anti-surface effectiveness.
This tracks with earlier framing of Wings of Theve's protagonist starting out on the aircraft
carrier Endurance — expect this to be one of the first aircraft you fly in the campaign.

## Su-57 Felon — a stealth rival

The second reveal introduces the **Su-57**, described as a cutting-edge stealth fighter
succeeding current air superiority jets, based on the real-world PAK FA program. Project Aces
specifically called out thrust-vectoring nozzles for "unmatched control." Framed as a
"mysterious enemy aircraft," this is very likely a Republic of Sotoa asset rather than
something the player unlocks early.

## Why this matters

This is the first time Bandai Namco has confirmed specific aircraft beyond the F-14A
pre-order bonus, and the framing — one player-aligned multirole jet, one enemy stealth
fighter — gives an early signal of how the roughly 30+ aircraft roster will be split between
allied and hostile forces.
